Cat Blood Type Guide

Understanding feline blood compatibility

A

Type A

~95% of cats in most breeds

The most common blood type. Type A cats can safely receive blood from other Type A donors. Must never receive Type B blood — it can cause fatal reactions.

✓ Can donate to A ✗ Cannot donate to B ⚠ Caution with AB
B

Type B

Common in British Shorthair, Devon Rex

Less common overall but prevalent in certain breeds. Type B cats have strong anti‑A antibodies, making cross‑type transfusion extremely dangerous.

✓ Can donate to B ✗ Cannot donate to A ⚠ Caution with AB
AB

Type AB

Very rare — ~1% of cats

The rarest feline blood type. AB cats are universal recipients and can receive from A, B, or AB donors, but their blood should only be given to other AB cats.

✓ Can receive from A, B, AB ✗ Should only donate to AB

⚠️ Important

Always consult a veterinarian before any blood transfusion. Cross‑matching tests should be performed even between same‑type cats.
